Checked In

Improving mental health and wellness at Harvard College

Mission Statement

Checked in is dedicated to bringing about meaningful and impactful change in mental wellbeing on college campuses through increasing awareness, accessibility and emotional intelligence.

Overview

Across America, mental health among college students has reached a breaking point. Faced with unprecedented challenges from technological development and societal pressures, Gen Z students aren't receiving the support that they deserve. This has caused an explosion in the number of students struggling with their own mental wellbeing, creating a crisis that can only be halted by providing students with the knowledge and tools to improve their mental health.


User Research

Prior to joining the project the team had collected data by administering surveys to Harvard College students. The data revealed that Harvard students are struggling with mental wellbeing (i.e., loneliness, depression, anxiety). Furthermore, it was discovered that these problems were aggravated by inefficient access to, and awareness of, on-campus mental health resources, including the efficacy of existing on-campus resources.
